01

Load or paste

Enter a target company and website. Toggle Live Search to pull public-source evidence via Tavily, or switch to Manual Notes and paste your own primary research from Perplexity, customer calls, or product docs.

Research mode

Live Search: combine lightweight public signals (when available) with any pasted research. The engine reasons over inputs — it does not fabricate sources.

02

Generate analysis

Click Run Discovery to populate the Research Log with live public-source evidence (Live Search mode only). Then click Generate Analysis to produce the nine MECE sections: product decomposition, adjacencies, incumbent and startup expansion, possibility vs likelihood, durability, evidence quality, falsifiability, and open diligence questions.

03

Export insights into PDF to add to diligence workstreams

Once analysis is generated, save the insight to local strategic memory or export a polished PDF memo ready to drop into a partner thread, deal room, or IC deck. Saved insights compound over time as the ecosystem moves and prior conclusions can be tested against subsequent events.

Differentiation Analysis

01 Product 02 Adjacencies 03 Incumbents 04 Startups 05 Possible vs Likely 06 Durability 07 Evidence 08 Falsifiability 09 Diligence Research Log
01

Product Decomposition

What the company actually does, in semi-technical terms. Product layer, workflow ownership, technical wedge, buyer, core dependency. No competitor trajectories here.
02

Strategic Adjacencies

Who is adjacent, why they are adjacent, why they matter. Identification only — depth analysis follows in the next two sections.
03

Incumbent Expansion Analysis

Per incumbent: technical capability, strategic incentive, distribution advantage, architectural and incentive constraints. No restatement of target product.
04

Startup Expansion Analysis

Per adjacent startup: wedge, likely roadmap direction, overlap / complement / acquisition logic.
05

Technically Possible vs Strategically Likely

The decisive distinction. What actors could technically build vs what they are actually incentivized to ship. Why strategic incentives may prevent technically possible moves.
06

Differentiation Durability

Final synthesis only. High / Medium / Low call, what is durable, what is vulnerable, confidence level. No restatement of prior sections.
07

Evidence Quality & Reasoning Signals

What the engine relied on. Strong evidence, weak evidence, inferred claims, missing evidence. No new strategic claims here.
08

What Would Change the Conclusion

Falsifiability of the analysis. What would strengthen the conclusion, what would weaken it, what evidence is needed next.
09

Open Diligence Questions

Next-step investor questions across product / architecture, GTM, buyer / budget, competitive dynamics.
